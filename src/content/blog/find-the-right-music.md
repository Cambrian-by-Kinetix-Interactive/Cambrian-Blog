If you've ever needed music for a project, you know the drill. You spend an hour digging through stock music sites, everything either sounds generic or costs way more than you expected, and the licensing terms are buried in a PDF nobody wants to read.

There's a better way to do this. Here's how to find the right music quickly and license it with confidence.

## Know What You Actually Need Before You Start Searching

Before you open any marketplace, get clear on three things:

1. **Where is the music going?** YouTube video, game, ad, podcast, film — the use case affects what kind of license you need.
2. **How long will you use it?** A one-off project is different from something that's going to be a permanent part of your brand.
3. **Do you need exclusivity?** If you're okay with other people using the same track, non-exclusive is cheaper. If you need it to be yours alone, go exclusive or buyout.

Knowing this upfront saves you from buying the wrong license and having to do it again.

## Search by Feel, Not Just Genre

Genre labels are pretty useless for finding the right music. "Electronic" could mean a hundred different things. Instead, search by mood — tense, uplifting, cinematic, energetic. Think about how you want your audience to feel when they hear it, and search from there.

## Read the License Before You Buy

This sounds obvious but most people skip it. Spend 60 seconds reading what you're actually buying. Can you use it commercially? Is there a limit on views or impressions? Can you edit it? These details matter and a good marketplace makes them easy to find upfront.

## AI Music Is Worth Considering

A lot of buyers still default to traditional stock music out of habit. But AI-generated music has gotten genuinely good, and it comes with clear commercial licensing because there are no legacy rights issues to navigate. For a lot of projects — especially content creation, games, and digital ads — it's a faster and more flexible option than traditional stock.
