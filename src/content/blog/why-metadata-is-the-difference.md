You uploaded a track. It sounds great. It sits there for weeks and nobody licenses it. What happened?

Nine times out of ten, the answer is metadata. The tags, descriptions, and categories attached to your music are what make it findable. Get them wrong and your track is invisible no matter how good it sounds.

## What Metadata Actually Does

When a buyer searches for music on Cambrian — or any marketplace — they're not browsing every track in the catalog. They're searching by mood, genre, tempo, use case, and keywords. Your metadata is what connects their search to your track.

Think of it like SEO for your music. The audio is the product, but the metadata is what gets it in front of the right people.

## The Tags That Actually Matter

Here's what you should focus on every time you upload:

**Genre** — Be specific. "Electronic" is too broad. "Lo-fi downtempo" or "cinematic ambient" tells buyers exactly what they're getting.

**Mood** — This is the most underrated tag. Buyers search by feel more than genre. Is your track tense? Hopeful? Mysterious? Energetic? Tag the emotion, not just the sound.

**Tempo** — A simple BPM tag helps buyers who need music to match a specific pacing — editors cutting to a beat, game designers syncing to gameplay.

**Use case** — Tag where this track works best. Background music for YouTube? Podcast intro? Game menu? Product ad? The more specific you are, the more likely a buyer finds you when they need exactly that.

**Instrumentation** — Piano-driven, synth-heavy, orchestral, guitar-based. These tags help buyers who know the sound they want but can't describe it by genre.

## Common Mistakes

**Being too vague** — "Chill" as your only mood tag means you're competing with thousands of tracks. Add specificity: "chill late-night lo-fi" narrows it down.

**Copying tags from other tracks** — Every track should be tagged individually based on what it actually sounds like. Bulk-applying the same tags across your catalog hurts discoverability for all of them.

**Skipping the description** — A short, natural-language description of your track helps with search. Write one or two sentences about what it sounds like and where it works. Buyers read these.

**Tagging what you wish it was** — Be honest about what the track sounds like, not what you intended. If you made something cinematic that ended up sounding more like ambient background music, tag it as ambient. Accuracy builds trust.

## Build the Habit

The best creators on Cambrian spend almost as much time on metadata as they do on the music itself. It's not busywork — it's the difference between a track that earns and a track that sits. Every upload is a chance to get better at it.
